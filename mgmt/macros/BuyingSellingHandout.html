Buying a Magic Item

You spend a week of downtime searching for old ladies looking to sell an antique sword they found in their attic, or searching Magical Craig's List for something that isn't a scam.

You may spend gold to more effectively search, you gain a +1 bonus for every 25gp spent on the search, up to a maximum bonus of +20.

Make a Charisma (Persuasion) or Intelligence (Investigation) check to determine the scope and quality of goods you can find in stock.

Based on the outcome of your check, you can choose one of the Magic Item Table options below, equal to or less than your check.

Shopping for Magic Items

Instead for looking to buy magic items from private owners, you can spend your downtime on buying magic items to browse the shops instead of rolling on tables.

This allows you to @Macro[Ly4iY0pKce6QIZgk]{use the macro} to see what is available. This gets you a much bigger list of items to choose from than the tables option. But rather than choosing a specfic table and focussing on a certain rarity, you are guaranteed a spread of rarities and tiers of items.

To compensate for the much larger selection, there are 2 downsides:

  1. Each of the 3 shops has a complication before you can buy its items.
    Read the text above the list of items to find out what this shop makes you do before you can buy items.
  2. When more than 1 player in a session uses the magic item shopping downtime and they wish to buy the same item, there is only 1 of that item. Each of the 3 shops lists a different way to determine who gets the item when players compete for that item.
((Since the item list is the same for all players in a session, if 1 player uses the macro that will allow other players to know what is available before they spend their downtimes, which helps them but also increases competition if players want the same item))

Selling a Magic Item

Different DMs will make different rulings on wether someone wants to buy or trade magic items and if they do, how much they are willing to pay.

Consider the #item-selling channel on the discord if you know how much you want for the item.

Or perhaps the #auction channel on the discord if you don't.

The items rolled on that table are the ones in stock, and can be purchased based on a randomly generated price.
